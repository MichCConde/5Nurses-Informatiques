Historical Background

Health informatics has its origins in the mid-20th century, with significant advancements in the 1950s.

Historical Background

Early pioneers like Joshua Lederberg and William S. Yamamoto recognized the potential of technology to streamline medical administrative tasks and improve patient record-keeping (Wired, 2024).

Over the decades, the field evolved from a focus on administrative efficiency to a comprehensive integration of technological advancements aimed at revolutionizing patient care and healthcare management (Wired, 2024).
